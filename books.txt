HISTORY:

A People's History of the United States 
Author: Howard Zinn
This book offers an alternative perspective on U.S. history, focusing on the experiences of ordinary people.

The History of the Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire
Author: Edward Gibbon
A comprehensive work that covers the history of the Roman Empire from the height of the Pax Romana to its fall.
 
The Rise and Fall of the Third Reich
Author: William L. Shirer
An in-depth examination of Nazi Germany, from its origins to the end of World War II.

A History of the Arab Peoples
Author: Albert Hourani
A comprehensive overview of the history and culture of the Arab world from pre-Islamic times to the present day.

The Diary of a Young Girl
Author: Anne Frank
The poignant and personal account of Anne Frank's experiences hiding from the Nazis during World War II.

The Second World War 
Author: Sir Winston Churchill
A six-volume series by the British Prime Minister who led his country during World War II, providing an insider's perspective on the conflict.

The Great Partition: The Making of India and Pakistan
Author: Yasmin Khan
Yasmin Khan explores the traumatic and complex history of the partition of India in 1947.

White Mughals: Love and Betrayal in Eighteenth-Century India
Author: William Dalrymple
Dalrymple's book explores the cultural interactions between British colonizers and Indian society in the 18th century.

Mughal Architecture: An Outline of Its History and Development (1526-1858)
Author: Ebba Koch
Focusing specifically on architecture, this book provides a detailed examination of Mughal architectural styles and developments over time.

Splendors of the Mughal Court
Author: Milo Cleveland Beach and Ebba Koch
Published in conjunction with an exhibition, this book showcases the artistic achievements of the Mughal court, including manuscripts, paintings, and textiles.



SCIENCE:

A Brief History of Time
Author: Stephen Hawking
This bestseller explores the nature of time, the universe, and black holes, written for a general audience.

Cosmos
Author: Carl Sagan
A classic that takes readers on a journey through space and time, exploring the wonders of the universe.

The Selfish Gene
Author: Richard Dawkins
Dawkins introduces the concept of the gene as a unit of natural selection, shaping our understanding of evolution.

Silent Spring
Author: Rachel Carson
A groundbreaking work that raised awareness about the environmental impact of pesticides, leading to the modern environmental movement.

The Double Helix 
Author: James D. Watson
This book recounts the discovery of the structure of DNA and the race to uncover the genetic code.

The Origin of Species
Author: Charles Darwin
Darwin's seminal work that laid the foundation for the theory of evolution by natural selection.

G del, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid 
Author: Douglas Hofstadter
An interdisciplinary exploration of mathematics, art, and music, delving into the nature of human thought.

The Elegant Universe 
Author: Brian Greene
Greene explains the complex theories of string theory and the nature of the universe in an accessible way.

Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind
Author: Yuval Noah Harari
While not strictly a science book, it explores the history of Homo sapiens from a scientific and anthropological perspective.

The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks 
Author: Rebecca Skloot
Blending science and narrative, this book tells the story of Henrietta Lacks and her immortal cells, which have been crucial in medical research.

Undying Affinity

Lahore: A Sentimental Journey

Pride and Prejudice
Author: Jane Austen
A classic novel that explores themes of love, class, and social expectations in 19th-century England.

To Kill a Mockingbird
Author: Harper Lee
A powerful novel that addresses racial injustice and moral growth in the American South.

1984 
Author: George Orwell
A dystopian novel that explores themes of totalitarianism, surveillance, and individuality.

The Great Gatsby
Author: F. Scott Fitzgerald
A portrayal of the American Dream and the Roaring Twenties, filled with themes of love, wealth, and social status.

Jane Eyre 
Author: Charlotte Bront 
A classic Gothic novel that follows the life of the orphaned Jane Eyre and her journey to independence and love.

One Hundred Years of Solitude
Author: Gabriel Garc a M rquez
A landmark novel in magical realism, exploring the multi-generational Buend a family in the fictional town of Macondo.

The Lord of the Rings
Author: J.R.R. Tolkien
A fantasy epic that follows the quest to destroy the One Ring and save Middle-earth from the dark forces of Sauron.




RELIGION AND SPIRTUALITY:

The Quran
The holy book of Islam, believed by Muslims to be the word of God as revealed to Prophet Muhammad. It serves as a guide for personal conduct, law, and spirituality.

Sahih al-Bukhari
One of the most authentic collections of hadiths (sayings and actions of Prophet Muhammad) compiled by Imam Bukhari. It is highly regarded in Sunni Islam.

Sahih Muslim
Another authoritative collection of hadiths, compiled by Imam Muslim. Like Sahih al-Bukhari, it is considered a crucial source for Islamic jurisprudence.

Riyadh as-Salihin(Gardens of the Righteous) 
Author: Imam Nawawi
A compilation of hadiths that focus on moral and ethical guidance, making it a widely read and respected book in the Islamic world.

Al-Muwatta
Author: Imam Malik ibn Anas
A collection of hadiths, legal opinions, and statements of the companions of Prophet Muhammad, serving as a foundational work in Islamic jurisprudence.

Ihya Ulum al-Din(The Revival of Religious Sciences) 
Author: Al-Ghazali
A comprehensive work on Islamic spirituality, ethics, and jurisprudence. It has had a profound impact on Islamic philosophy and Sufism.

Tafsir al-Jalalayn 
Author: Jalal ad-Din al-Mahalli and Jalal ad-Din as-Suyuti
A popular classical commentary on the Quran, providing insights into the meanings of the verses.

Fath al-Bari 
Author: Ibn Hajar al-Asqalani
A commentary on Sahih al-Bukhari, considered one of the most authoritative explanations of this collection of hadiths.

Kitab at-Tawhid
Author: Muhammad ibn Abd al-Wahhab
An influential work on Islamic theology and monotheism, emphasizing the oneness of God.

Al-Hidayah 
Author: Burhan al-Din al-Marghinani
A classical manual of Islamic law (fiqh), particularly in the Hanafi school, providing guidance on various aspects of life.



ART AND PHOTOGRAPHY:

The Story of Art 
Author: E.H. Gombrich
A widely acclaimed introduction to the history of art, covering various periods and styles in an accessible and engaging manner.

Ways of Seeing 
Author: John Berger
A groundbreaking work that explores how we perceive art and challenges traditional perspectives on art appreciation.

The Art Book 
Author: Phaidon Press
A comprehensive and visually stunning collection that features iconic works of art along with insightful commentary.

Camera Lucida 
Author: Roland Barthes
An influential and philosophical reflection on photography, exploring the emotional and personal impact of images.

On Photography
Author: Susan Sontag
A collection of essays that examines the cultural and philosophical implications of photography, questioning its role in society.

The Lives of the Artists 
Author: Giorgio Vasari
A foundational work in art history, providing biographies of Italian Renaissance artists and shaping the genre of artist biographies.

The Art of War 
Author: Sun Tzu
While not directly related to visual arts, this classic work on strategy and warfare has often been metaphorically applied to various aspects of life, including the art world.

The Letters of Vincent van Gogh
Author: Vincent van Gogh
A collection of letters written by the famous artist, providing insights into his thoughts, struggles, and artistic vision.

Camera Work 
Author: Alfred Stieglitz
A photographic journal published by Stieglitz from 1903 to 1917, featuring avant-garde photography and promoting photography as an art form.

The Power of Art
Author: Simon Schama
A book and a television series by historian Simon Schama, exploring the lives of eight artists and the impact of their work on art history.
