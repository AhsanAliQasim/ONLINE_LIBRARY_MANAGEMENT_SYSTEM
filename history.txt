HISTORY:


A People's History of the United States
Author: Howard Zinn
This book offers an alternative perspective on U.S. history, focusing on the experiences of ordinary people.

The History of the Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire
Author: Edward Gibbon
A comprehensive work that covers the history of the Roman Empire from the height of the Pax Romana to its fall.
 
The Rise and Fall of the Third Reich
Author: William L. Shirer
An in-depth examination of Nazi Germany, from its origins to the end of World War II.

A History of the Arab Peoples
Author: Albert Hourani
A comprehensive overview of the history and culture of the Arab world from pre-Islamic times to the present day.

The Diary of a Young Girl
Author: Anne Frank
The poignant and personal account of Anne Frank's experiences hiding from the Nazis during World War II.

The Second World War
Author: Sir Winston Churchill
A six-volume series by the British Prime Minister who led his country during World War II, providing an insider's perspective on the conflict.

The Great Partition: The Making of India and Pakistan
Author: Yasmin Khan
Yasmin Khan explores the traumatic and complex history of the partition of India in 1947.

White Mughals: Love and Betrayal in Eighteenth-Century India
Author: William Dalrymple
Dalrymple's book explores the cultural interactions between British colonizers and Indian society in the 18th century.

Mughal Architecture: An Outline of Its History and Development (1526-1858)
Author: Ebba Koch
Focusing specifically on architecture, this book provides a detailed examination of Mughal architectural styles and developments over time.

Splendors of the Mughal Court
Author: Milo Cleveland Beach and Ebba Koch
Published in conjunction with an exhibition, this book showcases the artistic achievements of the Mughal court, including manuscripts, paintings, and textiles.
